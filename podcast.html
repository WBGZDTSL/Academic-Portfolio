About This Podcast

What We Do Here

The Signal & The Noise is a research-focused podcast committed to clarity, rigor, and depth. Each episode features a structured conversation — typically 40–50 minutes — with a researcher, practitioner, or thinker whose work deserves wider attention in and beyond the academic community.

We do not simplify for the sake of accessibility. We explain for the sake of understanding. Guests are asked to describe not just what they found, but how they found it, what didn't work, and what remains genuinely uncertain.

The podcast is organized into three series:

  • NLP Series — language models, reasoning, and evaluation in depth
  • ML Tools Series — infrastructure, reproducibility, and research engineering
  • Open Science Series — publication norms, data sharing, and AI policy

New episodes are released every two weeks. Subscribe on your preferred platform below.

Reflection

Why I Started a Research Podcast

The initial impulse was frustration. Not with research itself, but with how research gets shared. Conferences are gate-kept. Preprints are noisy. Twitter moves too fast, and academic blogs move too slow.

What I wanted was a format that could hold a genuinely complex idea — and sit with it for forty minutes. A format that could ask a researcher not just what they found, but what surprised them, and what they would have done differently. Those are the conversations that happen in the corridor after a talk, never in the paper.

A podcast turned out to be the right medium. Audio is intimate. It tolerates ambiguity in ways that written text does not. And it reaches people who would never click through to an abstract.

Three years and forty episodes later, I still think it was the right decision. The show has led to collaborations I wouldn't have found otherwise, and to conversations I couldn't have predicted. That is reason enough.
